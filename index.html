I'm a Space Scientist exploring stars and their exotic adventures at the Institute of Astronomy, and a Research Fellow at Wolfson College, University of Cambridge.
Being the first person to obtain a doctorate in astrophysics from a Lebanese institution highlights my duty as a role model and inspires my gender advocacy work in STEM. Ever so eager to share my knowledge and passion, I'm an educator, mentor and a storyteller on my blog Scheherazade Speaks Science, which won the Cambridge University Public Engagement 2018 award.
Public speaking has been a thrilling and integral part of my professional life, whether on the TEDx stage or at international astronomy and interdisciplinary forums.

Scheherazade Speaks Science

It's a science communication platform that was awarded the Cambridge University Public Engagement Starter Fund 2018.
I write stories about space through storytelling and narrative formats to make it accessible and engaging to a wider audience. I also invite contributions from female scientists in all STEMM fields to promote their visibility.
In the tales of One Thousand and One Nights, Scheherazade the storyteller saves her life and the lives of other women with the power of story. In the 21st century, by engaging the public with her science she will spare herself and other women from bias and inequality.

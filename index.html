Who I am

An experienced Postdoctoral Researcher at the Institute of Astronomy, University of Cambridge, with a demonstrated history in research. The first person to obtain a Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Astronomy and Astrophysics from a Lebanese institution, the American University of Beirut. Course supervisor and Masters project mentor with a long and broad teaching experience. Recipient of several prestigious fellowships, awards and research grants. Skilled in Mathematical Modelling and proficient in English and Arabic. An enthusiastic team-worker, an excellent communicator and an activist in promoting gender equality and representation through supportive policies and practices. Active on various committees for an effective and strategic engagement in postdoc academic and social welfare issues in the University of Cambridge. An evolutionary professional with a Masters in Physics and a Bacherlors degree in Physics and Mathematics.

Research

To study stars and their interiors, we use evolutionary codes that model their structure and nucleosynthesis over time. During my Ph.D., I used the HYADES code (HYdrodynAmical coDe for Evolving Stars) under the supervision of Prof. Mounib El Eid. At the Institute of Astronomy, I use the Cambridge STARS code. My Ph.D. and postdoc research experience spans a wide range of stellar environments and phenomena. I have explored the evolution of single as well as binary stars and my work was focused, among various other things, on exploring reaction rates in stellar contexts and incorporating new reaction rates in astrophysical simulations. sections for various papers: CNO, novae, blue loops.

Teaching

I give supervisions for the Mathematics for Natural Sciences Tripos (NST) course at Churchill College, University of Cambridge. I supervise Masters projects at the Institute of Astronomy.
Previously, I taught Modern Physics, Astronomy, Mechanics and Electricity & Magnetism courses in the Physics department at American University of Beirut. I also supervised Electricity & Magnetism, Modern Physics and Mechanics labs at the American University of Beirut and the Lebanese American University, since 2006. Service & Engagement

  • Gender Equality

    • I am deeply committed to promoting meritocracy in academia through gender equality and representation in science. My role as University Representation and Policy Officer for STEMM in the Postdocs of Cambridge committee and being on the team that is responsible for achieving an Athena SWAN (a UK high-profile equality initiative) status for the IoA, I contribute to an action plan (link) that promotes gender equality, inclusion and career progression and success. These roles are ultimately breeding grounds for forging policies that are not only supportive of women entering science but also rising to the top echelons of scientific power, prestige and pay, circumventing a current problem known as the “leaky career pipeline”. I also chair an advisory Athena SWAN subcommittee on Anti-Bullying & Harassment which advised an action plan to effectively address this major issue that, our survey (link) shows, affects females disproportionately at the workplace.
    I represented the IoA at the Women in Astronomy IV conference, where I communicated IoA practices, institutional identity and values that embody its commitment to equality.

  • Outreach

    • I constantly engage in outreach events. I participated in the Cambridge Science Festival at the IoA open day, and I help run Window To The Stars, a colourful graphical interface to visualise the internal structure and evolution of a star, developed by Robert Izzard and Evert Glebbeek, during IoA open evenings. In Lebanon, I took part in the Science Days public events.

  • Community

    • I previously worked with MySchoolPulse, a non-profit NGO for schooling children with life-threatening illnesses. I also served on the Graduate Students Society at AUB. I'm on the LOC of several conferences and I currently serve on various committees.
